What is roulette?

Roulette is a mechanical device based on a project by the physicist Pascal that consisted of creating a perpetual motion machine, that is, an apparatus that, once started, would never stop. And although his experiment never worked, the prototype served as the basis for inventing a new type of game in which a rotating disc with numbered cavities was set in motion and then a ball was thrown into the disc.
This game, in which it was possible to place bets, soon became popular in the salons of the French aristocracy of the eighteenth century. The Blanc brothers were in charge of spreading the game, taking it to other European countries. The game became so popular that it crossed the Atlantic and came to the United States with some modifications, such as the introduction of the American eagle and the two zeros.

2. Place inside and outside bets

Roulette basically has two types of bets: inside and outside. Bets are divided into two types and are so named because, at the time of placing the bet, the place where the chips are placed can be towards the center (internal) or towards the periphery (external). Inside bets are made on one or more numbers, while outside bets are made on the quality of those numbers.
In inside bets we can bet directly on a number or a set of numbers. Thus, you can bet on the number 13 by placing the chip on top of its box, or on 13 and 14 by placing the chip on the line that divides the boxes of both numbers. In outside bets, on the other hand, we bet on black or red, or on whether the ball will land on the first, second or third dozen, or on whether the number will be odd or even.

4. Pay attention to draw patterns

If we assume that over a wide series of roulette spins, the probability of each number coming out is the same, it is worth paying attention and seeing which numbers came up the least and which ones more. The least drawn numbers may be the next numbers to be drawn, which would complete the series.
On the other hand, defying the saying that "lightning doesn't strike the same place twice," numbers that came out more may be more likely to come out again due to small imperfections in the mechanical device or simply due to varying temperature and pressure conditions in the place.
